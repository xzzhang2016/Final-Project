Life Expectancy at Birth is one of the most frequently used measure to study the demographic of countries in the world. It reflects the overall mortality level of a population, and measure the average number of years that a newborn is expected to live if mortality rate remains constant in the future (World Health Organization).

As you can see from the map, more developed regions of the world generally have higher life expectancies than less developed regions. However, some countries like Saudi Arabia and Kuwait have very high national income per capita but don't have high life expectancies. Alternatively, there are countries life China and Cuba that have relatively low national income but have reasonaly high life expectancies.

According to the Golbal Health Observatory (GHO) data, the average life expectancy at birth of the global population was 71.4 years in 2015.
